# Portfolio – AI context

## Design Context

### Users
- Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating technical background.
- Peers and potential collaborators (open source, contracts).
- Context: quick scan of projects/skills, optional deep read; contact for opportunities.

### Brand Personality
- **Technical, precise, confident.** Voice: clear and direct; no fluff. Emotional goal: convey competence and approachability without sounding corporate or generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Refined technical / editorial.** Not generic “AI slop”: avoid default Inter + purple gradients. Prefer a distinctive display font for headings and a readable body font; one strong accent color on a neutral base; subtle depth (grid, grain, or soft contrast). Light theme primary; dark mode optional later.
- **References (direction):** Developer portfolios that feel crafted (e.g. clear hierarchy, intentional typography, restrained motion). Avoid: cluttered dashboards, playful/playful-heavy, or purely brutalist.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity first** – Scannable layout, clear hierarchy, obvious CTAs (View Projects, Contact).
2. **Accessible by default** – Visible focus states, semantic HTML, skip link, labels on controls, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected.
3. **Consistent system** – Shared type scale, spacing, and components across public and admin.
4. **Performance** – Minimal layout shift; compositor-friendly motion (transform/opacity).
5. **Memorable detail** – One or two signature touches (e.g. typography, micro-motion, or background) that make the site feel intentional.
